How Have Indonesia's Trade Dynamics Evolved Over the Past Three Decades?

This project investigates how Indonesia's trade dynamics have evolved over the past three decades and identifies the key factors influencing its trade balance. By analyzing historical trends, key commodities, global trade partners, price-to-value relationships, and the impact of trade agreements, it provides a comprehensive understanding of Indonesia’s trade performance. Special attention is given to the effectiveness of economic policies like the Indonesia-Japan Economic Partnership Agreement (IJEPA).

Data Sources: World Bank API, UN Comtrade

  • Aims:
    • To analyze Indonesia's trade performance and its driving factors over the past three decades.
    • To identify key trade partners, commodities, and external factors influencing Indonesia’s trade balance.
    • To evaluate the effectiveness of economic policies and agreements like IJEPA.
  • Data:
    • World Bank data on exports, imports, trade balance, and commodity prices (1990–2022).
    • UN Comtrade data on key commodities, trade partners, and bilateral trade agreements (2000–2022).
  • Analytical Challenges:
    • Data Collection:
      • Inconsistent reporting formats across data sources (e.g., World Bank vs. UN Comtrade).
      • Difficulty in accessing up-to-date trade data due to time lags in reporting.
    • Data Transformation:
      • Standardizing trade data into a consistent tabular format suitable for analysis.
      • Integrating diverse datasets, such as country codes (ISO3) and HS codes for commodities.
      • Dealing with outliers and anomalous values in historical trade figures.
    • Data Visualization:
      • Balancing detail and clarity in visualizations, particularly for complex datasets (e.g., interactive maps).
      • Ensuring compatibility across platforms (e.g., JSON exports for web integration).
      • Addressing challenges in visualizing multi-dimensional data, such as trade value trends over time.
  • Conclusions:
    • Indonesia's trade balance has improved significantly since 2020, driven by increased exports and economic recovery post-COVID-19.
    • Key export commodities like palm oil and coal dominate revenue, while imports emphasize industrial reliance on foreign technology.
    • China, Japan, and ASEAN countries are vital trade partners, underscoring Indonesia's regional and global integration.
    • Price fluctuations in commodities like coal and palm oil significantly influence export revenues, highlighting Indonesia’s vulnerability to global markets.
    • The IJEPA agreement has positively impacted Indonesia's exports to Japan, particularly in industrial goods.

Chart 1: Indonesia Trade Data Visualization

This project visualizes Indonesia's trade indicators from 1990 to 2022, including exports, imports, and trade balance, using World Bank data. The chart highlights trends over time, offering insights into economic stability and trade dynamics.

Data Source: World Bank API

    • Data Collection: Retrieved data from the World Bank API for exports, imports, and trade balance (1990–2022).
    • Data Transformation: Cleaned and structured data into a tabular format, reshaped for visualization, ensuring Altair compatibility.
    • Data Visualization: Created an interactive Altair line chart with tooltips for trend exploration. Saved as JSON for web integration.
    • Notebook: Detailed steps and code are available in the Colab chart 1.
