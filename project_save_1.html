Introduction

Exploring the Impact of Trade on Indonesia’s Economic Stability

Project Overview:

The objective is to analyze the relationship between Indonesia’s trade and key economic indicators such as GDP, inflation, and foreign reserves. The study aims to uncover trends, identify key drivers of trade, and assess their influence on economic stability.

Data Sources:

  • UN Comtrade
  • World Bank
  • IMF

Chart 1: Indonesia Trade Data Visualization

This project visualizes Indonesia's trade indicators from 1990 to 2022, including exports, imports, and trade balance, using World Bank data. The chart highlights trends over time, offering insights into economic stability and trade dynamics.

Data Source: World Bank API

  • Purpose: To analyze Indonesia's trade performance over time, providing insights into economic stability and the influence of trade dynamics.
  • Key Insights:
    • Steady Growth in Exports: Exports have grown consistently, rising from approximately $29 billion in 1990 to over $323 billion in 2022, showcasing Indonesia's increasing integration into global trade markets.
    • Growth in Imports: Imports have also risen significantly, from around $27 billion in 1990 to nearly $277 billion in 2022, reflecting Indonesia's growing domestic demand and industrialization.
    • Fluctuating Trade Balance:
      • Surpluses are more frequent before 2012, with the largest surplus of approximately $47 billion in 2022.
      • Deficits are seen from 2012 to 2019, with the largest deficit of around $11 billion in 2018.
    • Impact of Global Events:
      • Sharp declines in exports and imports in 1998 and 2009 align with the Asian Financial Crisis and the Global Financial Crisis, respectively.
      • A rebound in 2021 and 2022 indicates recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic and increased global commodity demand.
    • Trade Balance Shift: A return to a strong surplus in 2020–2022 highlights the influence of rising export revenues, particularly in commodities, outpacing import growth.
  • Methodology:
    • Data Collection: Retrieved data from the World Bank API for three indicators: Exports (US$), Imports (US$), and Trade Balance (US$), covering 1990–2022.
    • Data Transformation: Processed the data into a tabular format, reshaped it for visualization, and ensured compatibility with Altair.
    • Data Visualization: Created an interactive Altair line chart with tooltips to display trends for exports, imports, and trade balance. Saved the chart as a JSON file for web integration.
    • Notebook: Detailed steps and code are available in my Project 1 Notebook.

Chart 3: Indonesia's Export and Import Partner Countries

This section visualizes Indonesia's trade relationships with its partner countries, highlighting total export and import values for the years 2012–2022. The interactive maps provide insights into trade distribution across the globe.

Export Partner Countries

This interactive map shows the total exports (US$) from Indonesia to its partner countries for selected years. The darker shades represent higher export values.

Data Source: UN Comtrade

Import Partner Countries

This interactive map illustrates the total imports (US$) to Indonesia from its partner countries for selected years. The darker shades indicate higher import values.

Data Source: UN Comtrade

  • Purpose: To analyze Indonesia's trade relationships with partner countries, understanding the global distribution of trade flows and identifying key export and import partners.
  • Key Insights:
    • Top Export Partners: China is Indonesia’s largest export destination in 2022, with $65.9 billion in exports, followed by the United States ($28.2 billion) and Japan ($24.8 billion). Other key export partners include India, Malaysia, and Singapore, highlighting a mix of global and regional trade connections. Commodities like palm oil and coal play a significant role in driving these export values.
    • Top Import Partners: China also leads Indonesia’s imports at $67.7 billion in 2022, followed by Singapore ($19.4 billion), Japan ($17.1 billion), and Malaysia ($12.4 billion). The United States, Republic of Korea, and Thailand are other significant contributors, reflecting reliance on foreign machinery, electronics, and raw materials.
    • Trade Distribution: Indonesia’s trade network shows strong regional ties with ASEAN countries like Singapore, Malaysia, and Thailand, while also maintaining critical relationships with major global economies such as China, the United States, and Japan.
    • Economic Trends: The prominence of China in both exports and imports reflects Indonesia's deep integration into the global economy. Regional trade partnerships, especially within ASEAN, continue to play a crucial role in sustaining trade flows.
  • Methodology:
    • Data Collection: Retrieved global trade data from the UN Comtrade database, focusing on Indonesia's trade with partner countries, including trade flows, partner names, and values (US$).
    • Data Analysis:
      • Filtered data for exports and imports by partner countries for the years 2012–2022.
      • Calculated the total trade values (US$) for each country per year.
    • Data Transformation: Integrated partner data with a world map using country ISO3 codes and reshaped data for visualization compatibility.
    • Data Visualization: Developed interactive maps using Vega-Lite to:
      • Highlight export partners and their total trade values (US$).
      • Showcase import partners and their total trade values (US$).

Chart 5: IJEPA Implementation Impact in 2008 using Difference-in-Differences

This chart evaluates the impact of the Indonesia-Japan Economic Partnership Agreement (IJEPA) using a Difference-in-Differences (DiD) analysis, with India as the control group. The analysis compares Indonesia's trade metrics with Japan before and after IJEPA implementation, using India's trade metrics with Japan as a baseline.

Data Source: UN Comtrade

  • Purpose: To estimate the causal effect of IJEPA on Indonesia's trade performance by comparing it to a control group (India).
  • Key Insights:
    • Indonesia's trade volume with Japan experienced a significant increase post-IJEPA, compared to India's relatively stable trend.
    • The DiD analysis suggests that IJEPA positively influenced Indonesia's trade metrics with Japan, particularly in exports.
    • India's trade with Japan provides a robust counterfactual, highlighting the agreement's potential causal impact.
  • Methodology:
    • Data Collection: Retrieved trade data from UN Comtrade, focusing on bilateral trade metrics between:
      • Indonesia and Japan (Treatment Group)
      • India and Japan (Control Group)
    • Difference-in-Differences Analysis:
      • Trade metrics analyzed: total trade volume, export values, and import values.
      • Pre-IJEPA period: 2000–2007; Post-IJEPA period: 2008–2022.
      • Estimated the impact of IJEPA by calculating the interaction term between Indonesia and post-IJEPA years.
    • Visualization: Line chart with two series:
      • Indonesia-Japan trade (treatment)
      • India-Japan trade (control)
      Vertical dashed line indicating IJEPA implementation in 2008.
