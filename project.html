Introduction

Exploring the Impact of Trade on Indonesia’s Economic Stability

Project Overview:

The objective is to analyze the relationship between Indonesia’s trade and key economic indicators such as GDP, inflation, and foreign reserves. The study aims to uncover trends, identify key drivers of trade, and assess their influence on economic stability.

Data Sources:

  • UN Comtrade
  • World Bank
  • IMF

Chart 1: Indonesia Trade Data Visualization

This project visualizes Indonesia's trade indicators from 1990 to 2022, including exports, imports, and trade balance, using World Bank data. The chart highlights trends over time, offering insights into economic stability and trade dynamics.

Data Source: World Bank API

  • Purpose: To evaluate Indonesia's trade performance from 1990 to 2022, focusing on the interplay between exports, imports, and trade balance.
  • Key Insights:
    • Exports: Rose from $29B (1990) to $323B (2022), showcasing global trade integration.
    • Imports: Climbed from $27B (1990) to $277B (2022), fueled by domestic demand and industrial growth.
    • Trade Balance: Surpluses peaked at $47B (2022); deficits occurred from 2012–2019, largest at $11B (2018).
    • Global Events: Major dips during the Asian Financial Crisis (1998) and Global Financial Crisis (2009); strong rebounds post-COVID-19 (2021–2022).
  • Methodology:
    • Data Collection: Retrieved data from the World Bank API for exports, imports, and trade balance (1990–2022).
    • Data Transformation: Cleaned and structured data into a tabular format, reshaped for visualization, ensuring Altair compatibility.
    • Data Visualization: Created an interactive Altair line chart with tooltips for trend exploration. Saved as JSON for web integration.
    • Notebook: Detailed steps and code are available in the Project 1 Notebook.

Chart 5: IJEPA Implementation Impact in 2008 using Difference-in-Differences

This chart evaluates the impact of the Indonesia-Japan Economic Partnership Agreement (IJEPA) using a Difference-in-Differences (DiD) analysis, with India as the control group.

Data Source: UN Comtrade

  • Purpose: To estimate IJEPA's causal effect on Indonesia's trade performance with Japan.
  • Key Insights:
    • IJEPA led to a significant increase in Indonesia’s exports to Japan compared to India, the control group.
    • The agreement positively influenced trade, particularly in exports.
  • Methodology:
    • Data Collection: Bilateral trade data between Indonesia, Japan, and India from UN Comtrade.
    • DiD Analysis: Compared pre- and post-IJEPA trade volumes (2000–2007 and 2008–2022).
    • Data Visualization: Line charts with vertical markers for the 2008 IJEPA implementation.
